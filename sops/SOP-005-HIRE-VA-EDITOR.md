# SOP-005: Hiring a VA Video Editor (and Other Contractors)

**Version**: 1.0
**Owner**: Founder
**When to hire**: When you personally have >5 edited videos in queue OR when your time is better spent on strategy than editing.

---

## 1. Write the Job Post

Post on these platforms (best first):
1. **OnlineJobs.ph** — Filipino VAs, English-speaking, $3–$8/hr, BEST for video editors
2. **Upwork** — global, $10–$25/hr for skilled editors
3. **Facebook groups** — "YouTube Editors United", "Video Editing Jobs"
4. **Fiverr** — for per-piece gigs when starting
5. **Toptal / Contra** — for premium editors (scale phase)

### Job Post Template

```
TITLE: YouTube Video Editor for Faceless Channels — Long-term Work

We run a growing portfolio of faceless YouTube channels (finance/tech/self-improvement niches) and are looking for a reliable long-term video editor.

REQUIREMENTS:
- Proficient in CapCut OR Premiere Pro OR DaVinci Resolve
- Experience editing faceless/YouTube automation videos (SEND PORTFOLIO)
- Can add B-roll, text overlays, transitions, captions, sound effects
- 24–48 hour turnaround per video
- Good English comprehension
- Stable internet + powerful enough computer for 1080p exports
- Willing to do a PAID test edit ($15 for 1-min test)

PAY:
- $10–$20 per finished 8–12 min video (based on skill)
- OR $4–$7/hr, 20–40 hrs/week at scale
- Weekly payout via Wise/PayPal
- Raises + bonuses for videos that hit >100k views

TO APPLY:
1. Send portfolio of 3 YouTube-style edits you've done
2. Answer: What editor do you use?
3. Answer: What's your turnaround for a 10-min video?
4. Start your message with "YACC-EDIT" so I know you read this.

NO AGENCIES. Individual freelancers only.
```

---

## 2. Filter Applicants

- Auto-reject anyone who didn't start with "YACC-EDIT" (didn't read the post)
- Reject anyone without portfolio links
- Skim portfolios — skip anyone whose edits look like 2012 Windows Movie Maker
- Pick 5–8 candidates for test edit

---

## 3. Paid Test Edit ($15)

Send shortlisted candidates:
- A 1–2 min sample voiceover MP3
- The script
- A sample reference video ("edit it in THIS style")
- The channel's brand colors/fonts
- 48-hour deadline

Pay every candidate who submits (even bad ones — respectful, builds reputation).

### Evaluate on:
- [ ] Pacing (cuts on beat, no dead air)
- [ ] B-roll relevance (does footage match what's being said?)
- [ ] Caption styling (font, size, timing)
- [ ] Audio levels (music vs VO balance)
- [ ] Transitions tasteful (not overdone)
- [ ] On-time delivery
- [ ] Communication quality

Pick top 1–2 candidates.

---

## 4. Probation Period (first 10 videos)

- Start with 3 videos first (paid), evaluate
- If good, commit to 10 videos at agreed rate
- Give specific feedback EVERY video for first 10 videos (use Loom for screen-record feedback)
- Use a scorecard 1–10: pacing, B-roll, captions, audio, on-time
- Fire fast if:
  - Misses deadlines 2x without heads-up
  - Quality doesn't improve after 2 rounds of feedback
  - Rude/difficult communication

---

## 5. Onboarding (first day)

Send new hire:
1. **Welcome packet PDF** (see `team/onboarding/`):
   - Channel specs document (brand, fonts, colors, style)
   - SOPs relevant to their role (SOP-003 for writers, SOP-004 for thumbnails, etc.)
   - Folder access (Google Drive with B-roll, music, templates)
   - Reference videos (3 top-performing videos to mimic)
   - Trello/Notion invite
   - Communication channel (Discord/Slack)
   - Payment schedule and method
2. **Kickoff call** (15 min, video on) — meet them, show examples, set expectations
3. **First assignment** — give 1 video, give detailed feedback after

---

## 6. Ongoing Management

- **Daily**: check Trello for cards moving; nudge if stuck
- **Weekly**: give 5-minute feedback summary on Slack
- **Per video**: approve or send back with time-coded notes ("1:23 B-roll doesn't match, replace with X")
- **Bonuses**: $20–$50 bonus for any video they edit that hits >100k views in 30 days (aligns incentives)
- **Raises**: after 3 months of solid work, raise rate by $2–$3/video or $1/hr
- **Reviews**: formal review at 30/60/90 days

---

## 7. Standard Rates (2026 benchmark)

| Role | Rate | Where to hire |
|---|---|---|
| Video editor (CapCut, mid-tier) | $10–$15/video (8–10 min) | OnlineJobs.ph |
| Video editor (Premiere, advanced) | $20–$40/video | Upwork |
| Script writer | $5–$15/script | OnlineJobs.ph, Upwork, ProBlogger |
| Thumbnail designer | $3–$8/thumb (set of 2) | Fiverr, Upwork |
| VA uploader/SEO | $3–$5/hr | OnlineJobs.ph |
| Channel manager | $500–$1,500/mo | Upwork, LinkedIn |

---

## 8. Payment Setup

- **Best for Filipino VAs**: **Wise.com** (lowest fees) or **PayPal**
- **Always pay weekly for first month**, then switch to bi-weekly after trust is built
- **Never pay upfront** for bulk work — pay on delivery
- Get receipts / invoices for accounting
- Issue 1099s (US) if paying >$600/yr to US-based contractors

---

## 9. Red Flags (fire immediately)

- Plagiarizes work (uses someone else's portfolio, steals footage)
- Shares your content/SOPs with other clients
- Lies about hours or work done
- Harasses other team members
- Demands payment before delivering
- Goes MIA for >48h without warning
- Signs of account sharing (work style drastically different suddenly)

Trust your gut. Hire slow, fire fast.
