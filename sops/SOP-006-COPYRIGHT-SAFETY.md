# SOP-006: Copyright & Content Safety

**Version**: 1.0
**Owner**: Everyone — enforced by Manager at upload step
**Why**: One strike = warning. Three strikes = channel deleted. No exceptions, no shortcuts.

---

## The Golden Rule

**If you didn't make it OR don't have a license, DON'T USE IT.**

"YouTube automation" =/= "steal other people's content." We produce ORIGINAL, transformed content.

---

## ✅ SAFE to Use (Proven)

### Video footage (B-roll)
- Pexels.com — free, no attribution required
- Pixabay.com — free
- Unsplash.com — free (photos)
- Videvo.net — free tier (check license per clip)
- Storyblocks / Artgrid / Epidemic Sound — paid, fully licensed
- Your own screen recordings
- AI-generated footage (Runway, Pika, Sora)
- Government / public domain footage (NASA, US military, pre-1928 works)

### Music
- YouTube Audio Library (FREE, built into Studio)
- Epidemic Sound (paid ~$15/mo, fully licensed)
- Artlist (paid)
- Pixabay Music, Chosic (free)
- AI-generated music (Suno, Udio) — verify terms at time of use

### Images
- Pexels, Pixabay, Unsplash
- Wikipedia Creative Commons (attribute)
- Canva free/pro elements (read license — most fine for YouTube, but not for resale as templates)
- AI-generated (DALL-E, Midjourney) — you own the output per their ToS

### Voice
- Your own voice
- Licensed AI voices (ElevenLabs paid tier = license for YouTube monetization)
- Hired voice actor (with contract granting commercial rights)
- ElevenLabs voice clone of YOUR voice only (NEVER clone a celebrity or YouTuber)

### Facts & Ideas
- Facts aren't copyrightable — you can always report the same facts/stats AS LONG AS YOU WRITE IT IN YOUR OWN WORDS
- Cite sources, rephrase, don't copy-paste paragraphs

---

## ❌ NEVER Use (Ban List)

- Other YouTubers' content (clips, reactions, "fair use" arguments are dangerous — even if legal, YouTube's automated Content ID will flag you)
- TV shows, movies, Netflix clips
- Sports highlights
- Music from major labels (even 2 seconds!)
- Celebrity voices / impressions for AI clones
- Tiktok/Reels clips you downloaded
- Articles rewritten verbatim by AI (still copyright infringement if structure/substance identical)
- Stock footage marked "editorial use only" (for ads/revenue videos)
- Any content with watermarks (Shutterstock, Getty, etc.) unless you paid for license
- Kids' characters / Disney / Marvel / anime characters (vigorously enforced)
- Clip art from random Google image searches (assume copyrighted)
- Spotify / Apple Music audio of any song

---

## ⚠️ "Fair Use" — Use with Extreme Caution

Fair use is a DEFENSE, not a right. YouTube almost always sides with the claimant first.

Fair use generally covers:
- Criticism & review
- Parody
- News reporting
- Teaching/scholarship

Even if you think it's fair use:
- Use <5 seconds
- Heavily transform it (crop, zoom, add commentary, voiceover)
- Don't use the "heart" of the work
- EXPECT a claim, and be ready to dispute
- When in doubt: DON'T

We avoid fair-use-reliant content at MVP stage.

## Content ID Claims — What To Do

If you get a **Content ID claim** (different from a copyright strike — less severe):

1. Don't panic — claims usually just mean revenue goes to rights holder, not a strike
2. Open YouTube Studio → Content → click the video → Copyright claims
3. Options:
   - **Mute the segment** (if music) — fastest fix
   - **Trim/cut out** the segment (re-render and replace if needed)
   - **Dispute** ONLY if you're 100% sure you have rights or it's fair use
   - **Appeal** if dispute was rejected and you're still sure
4. If the claim blocks the video worldwide, fix it immediately (revenue lost)

### Dispute template (when you truly have rights):
```
I have a license for this content from [Platform Name, e.g. Epidemic Sound / Storyblocks].
My license number is [XXX]. The content appears between [start] and [end].
Please release the claim. I can provide proof of license upon request.
```

## Copyright STRIKES — Emergency Response

A strike is serious. 3 = channel deleted.

**Immediate actions**:
1. **DO NOT delete the video yet** (if you delete within 7 days you lose the ability to appeal; if you DO successfully appeal the strike goes away — weigh carefully)
2. Screenshot the strike details
3. If you're clearly in the wrong → delete the video, take it as a warning, fix your process
4. If you believe it's a mistake/abuse:
   - File a counter-notification (requires legal name/address/phone)
   - Wait 10 business days for claimant to act
   - If no legal action from claimant, strike is released
5. **Mandatory**: Complete YouTube's "Copyright School" if offered
6. **Pause uploads on that channel for 48 hours**, audit recent videos for similar issues
7. If strike #2 — stop all uploads, do a full channel audit, consider moving to next channel
8. If strike #3 — export all data, the channel will be terminated; prepare to move subs to new channel via community tab post if still possible

Strikes expire after **90 days** if you complete Copyright School and get no more.

## Pre-Upload Checklist (Every Video)

Before clicking publish, uploader confirms:
- [ ] All B-roll from approved sources (Pexels, paid libraries, AI-generated)
- [ ] Music from YouTube Audio Library or Epidemic/Artlist subscription
- [ ] No clips of other YouTubers/TV/movies
- [ ] AI voice is from ElevenLabs paid tier (we have commercial license)
- [ ] Script is original (AI-assisted but human-edited, no plagiarism)
- [ ] No celebrity voice clones
- [ ] No logos/brands in a way that implies endorsement (e.g., you can SHOW a Nike shoe in B-roll but can't say "Nike recommends this")
- [ ] Any stock footage subscription is currently active (not expired)
- [ ] If using public figure footage (politician speech, historical), <5 sec and transformative commentary over it

## Disclaimer Requirements

Add these disclaimers to video descriptions when relevant:

**Financial videos**:
> This video is for educational and entertainment purposes only. This is not financial advice. Always do your own research and consult a licensed financial advisor before making investment decisions. Some links may be affiliate links, meaning we earn a small commission at no cost to you.

**Health videos**:
> This video is for educational purposes only and is not a substitute for professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Always seek the advice of your physician or qualified health provider with any questions regarding a medical condition.

**Legal/business videos**:
> This content is for educational purposes only and does not constitute legal advice. Consult a licensed attorney for advice specific to your situation.

## Quarterly Audit

Every 90 days:
1. Pull list of all videos on all channels
2. Spot-check 20% of videos for any B-roll/music that might now be flagged (Content ID claims can hit retroactively!)
3. Review active music/stock subscriptions — renew or replace footage if lapsing
4. Update ban list as new lawsuit precedents come up (e.g., if AI-generated voices of real people get banned — adapt immediately)
5. Train team on any new rules
