# Voiceover Generation Guide (ElevenLabs / TTS Best Practices)

This isn't a prompt per se, but a guide + system prompt for consistent, human-sounding VO.

---

## Recommended ElevenLabs Settings

| Setting | Value | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Voice | Choose per channel (Adam/Daniel/Rachel/Charlotte are solid defaults) | Consistency = brand |
| Model | Eleven Turbo v2.5 or v2.5 (latest) | Best quality |
| Stability | 0.35–0.45 | Lower = more expressive, varied. Higher = monotone. |
| Similarity boost | 0.75–0.90 | Keep it sounding like the voice actor |
| Style exaggeration | 0.30–0.50 | Adds emotional range without going crazy |
| Speaker boost | ON | Helps audio cut through music |

Generate 2–3 versions of each paragraph, pick the best. Use the "regen" button on bad sentences.

---

## Script Preparation Before Pasting

Before pasting into TTS, CLEAN the script:
1. **REMOVE** all visual cues like `[B-ROLL: ...]`, `[SCREENSHOT: ...]`
2. **REMOVE** metadata block (titles/tags/thumbnail ideas)
3. **REMOVE** timestamps and section markers like "HOOK:", "INTRO:", "BODY:"
4. Add punctuation for pacing:
   - Use `—` (em dash) for natural pauses mid-sentence
   - Use `...` for dramatic pauses
   - Use line breaks for sentence breaks
5. Spell out numbers (ElevenLabs sometimes misreads numerals):
   - "1,000,000" → "one million"
   - "$47,000" → "forty-seven thousand dollars"
   - "1984" → "nineteen eighty-four" (year) vs "one thousand nine hundred eighty-four" (number)
6. Spell out acronyms on first use then switch: "Artificial Intelligence, or A-I...", then "AI" later
7. Mark emphasis with CAPS or bold? Don't — ElevenLabs handles natural emphasis; over-mark it sounds forced.

---

## Pronunciation Fixes

If ElevenLabs mispronounces a word:
1. Open the Pronunciation Library in ElevenLabs
2. Add respelling phonetically:
   - "ChatGPT" → "chat G P T"
   - "SaaS" → "sass"
   - "Nikola Tesla" → confirm it says "NIK-oh-la", not "ni-KOH-la"
3. Alternative: respell directly in script:
   - "entrepreneur" → "on-truh-pruh-NOOR" won't work; instead rephrase sentence to avoid it, or add a short audio clip of correct pronunciation

---

## Post-Generation Quality Check

Listen ALL THE WAY THROUGH once before sending to editor. Flag/regen:
- [ ] Mumbled words / gibberish
- [ ] Wrong emphasis ("re-CORD" vs "RE-cord")
- [ ] Sudden voice changes (rare, but happens)
- [ ] Incorrect pronunciation of names/brands
- [ ] Breath sounds in wrong places (use ElevenLabs auto-breath reduction)
- [ ] Audio clipping / distortion (turn down output volume slightly)
- [ ] Pace too fast (>170 WPM) or too slow (<130 WPM)

## Example "Clean" VO Script (input to ElevenLabs)

```
I just got an email from YouTube I never thought I'd get.
[slight pause — use comma or new paragraph]
They were asking me to speak at their creator summit.
But here's the kicker — three years ago, I was dead broke.
I was working a dead-end job making $14 an hour...
...sleeping on my cousin's couch...
...and wondering if I'd ever make anything of my life.
In this video, I'm going to show you the exact three things I did to go from that couch...
...to running a 7-figure YouTube business.
And no, it wasn't luck. And it wasn't rich parents.
```

Note the natural breaks — short paragraphs = natural pauses. The TTS engine reads short paragraphs with better rhythm than long blocks.

---

## Background Music Mixing Notes (for editor)

- Background music should be **-20 to -24dB** relative to voice
- Duck music -6dB during key moments (revelations, emotional beats)
- Fade music in/out at start/end over 1.5 seconds
- Use instrumental lo-fi / ambient / corporate beats matching niche:
  - Finance/tech: upbeat electronic, light synth
  - Self-improvement: ambient piano, lo-fi
  - History/documentary: orchestral, cinematic
  - Scary/shocking stories: tense drones (low volume!)
