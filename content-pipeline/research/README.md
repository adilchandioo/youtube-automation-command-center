# 🔬 Stage 2: Research

Goal: Before writing the script, collect facts, stats, stories, sources, and competitor angles. Output = a research brief that the writer can turn into a script in 60 minutes.

Owner: Script Writer
SLA: 6 hours max per video from card entering "Researching"

### 3. Facts & Stats (with sources)
Find 5-10 concrete stats/data points. FORMAT:
> "Stat/fact here" — [Source Name](URL)

Best sources:
- Statista
- Wikipedia (check citations)
- Pew Research, Bureau of Labor Statistics, World Bank
- Niche publications (Forbes, Business Insider, Mayo Clinic, etc.)
- Academic papers (Google Scholar)
- YouTube competitor videos (cross-check!)
- Reddit/Quora anecdotes (story ideas, not factual sources)

NEVER make up stats. If you can't find a stat, don't use one. If a stat is from a random blog with no citation, don't use it.

### 4. Stories & Examples
Find 2-3 concrete anecdotes/examples to humanize the video:
- Success story (person who did X and got Y result)
- Failure story (person who did X wrong and failed — lessons)
- Personal anecdote: if you/host have one, include it
- Celebrity/well-known example (e.g., "Warren Buffett does X")

### 5. Visual Assets to Find / Note
- [ ] Are there screenshots I should use (app interfaces, earnings, charts)?
- [ ] What B-roll topics will editor need? (list: "office work, coding, cash, etc.")
- [ ] Any graphs/charts I can sketch for editor?
- [ ] Public domain footage sources (e.g., NASA, C-SPAN)?

### 6. Affiliate / Product Tie-ins
- Products/tools mentioned in the video?
- Affiliate links available for each?
- Where in script should they be mentioned (naturally, not forced)?

## AI-Assisted Research Workflow (faster)

Using Perplexity.ai or Claude:
1. Ask: "What are the most recent stats on {{topic}} in 2026? Include sources."
2. Ask: "What are the 3 most common mistakes people make with {{topic}}?"
3. Ask: "Are there any counterintuitive or controversial findings about {{topic}}?"
4. Cross-check ALL facts AI gives you (AI hallucinates — verify every stat by opening the source)
