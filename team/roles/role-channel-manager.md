# Role: Channel Manager

**Reports to**: Founder
**Rate**: $800–$1,500/mo (per 3-5 channels managed)
**Hours**: Full-time or part-time, async OK
**Location**: Remote

---

## What You'll Do (you run the show day-to-day)
- Own content pipeline: ensure videos move from idea → publish on schedule
- Assign cards in Trello/Notion, follow up on stuck team members
- Do script approval (first pass — founder does final approval at first, full approval after trust)
- Do daily QA of scheduled videos (SOP-001)
- Run weekly review meeting (SOP-002), produce weekly report
- Manage the team (writer, editor, designer, uploader) — give feedback, escalate issues
- Monitor analytics daily, flag viral videos / underperformers
- Suggest new video ideas, experiments, process improvements
- First line of response for YouTube warnings/strikes
- Send weekly P&L update to founder

## Required Skills
- 1+ year running YouTube channels (portfolio of channels you've managed)
- Deep understanding of YouTube algorithm (CTR, AVD, retention curves)
- Project management skills (can run a distributed team)
- Good written English
- Data-literate (can read YouTube analytics and act on insights)
- Calm under pressure (strikes, glitches, viral moments)
- Self-directed — doesn't wait to be told what to do
- Can hire/train new VAs as team grows

## KPIs (you're judged on these)
| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Videos published per channel per week | 3 long-form |
| Average CTR across your channels | >7% |
| Average AVD across your channels | >40% |
| Subscriber growth month-over-month | +15% |
| Revenue growth month-over-month | +20% |
| On-time delivery rate (pipeline healthy) | >95% |
| Founder time needed from you | <1hr/day |

## Decision Autonomy
You CAN do without asking:
- Assign/reassign work
- Give feedback/coaching to team
- Swap thumbnails/titles on underperforming videos
- Kill low-score ideas
- Run A/B tests
- Respond to routine comments/emails

You MUST ask founder before:
- Hiring/firing
- Spending >$100
- Changing channel brand/name/niche
- Responding to legal/strike notices
- Signing any contract/sponsorship deal
- Posting controversial content

## Growth Path
Best channel managers can become COO/Operations Director with equity/bonus as the business scales.
